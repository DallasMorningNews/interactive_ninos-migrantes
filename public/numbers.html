    1. Are more immigrants being detained at the border?

    No. Overall apprehensions on the Southwest border are a quarter of their 2000 peak.

    A recent report from the Pew Research Center found that the number of unauthorized immigrants living in the country has not increased since 2009.

    2. What countries are the migrants coming from?

    Since 2014, 3 out of every 4 unaccompanied minors encountered by the Border Patrol have come from El Salvador, Honduras or Guatemala. About a quarter came from Mexico.

    Almost 90 percent of the children apprehended with their families came from El Salvador, Honduras or Guatemala. Seven percent were Mexicans.

    Roxana, Antonio and Cesar came from El Salvador in 2015 and 2016.

    3. Why are they leaving home?

    Experts point to the increasing crime levels in the region, poverty and food insecurity as common factors pushing people to flee these countries. Reuniting with a parent already in the U.S. motivates many as well.

    4. How many minors who came without a parent or legal guardian have been released to sponsors in North Texas?

    More than 3,700 children and teenagers have been placed with parents, uncles, friends and other sponsors in the region.

    5. What happens to families and unaccompanied minors facing deportation in Dallas immigration court?

    FAMILIES WITH CHILDREN

    Eight out of 10 families have no legal assistance in Dallas Immigration Court, according to the Transactional Records Access Clearinghouse at Syracuse University. Nationwide, nearly half have representation.

    The largest group in Dallas comes from El Salvador.

    César and his family are still struggling to find representation.

    UNACCOMPANIED MINORS

    There is more legal help for juveniles than for families in Dallas. Roxana is an example. She has a lawyer thanks to a government grant.

    Nationwide, juveniles also receive more attention than in Dallas: 54 percent vs. 37 percent, according to the Transactional Records Access Clearinghouse at Syracuse University.

    As with families, the largest juvenile group in Dallas comes from El Salvador.

    6. How many migrants who came as unaccompanied minors have been physically removed?

    In fiscal 2014 and 2015, nearly 4,000 people, according to the federal immigration enforcement agency.

    Ana Azpurua and Dianne Solis
